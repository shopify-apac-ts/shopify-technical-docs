Shopify Collective flows for retailers and suppliers

Terms

The product page uses the same role definitions as the source Markdown.

Source: architecture.md
SupplierOwns source products, shares them through Collective, and fulfills orders directly to the end customer.
RetailerImports supplier products, sells them on its storefront, and handles the customer relationship.
Price list / catalogSupplier-managed products and commercial terms shared with retailers.
Cost priceSupplier-side amount the retailer owes the supplier for a Collective product sale.
Retail priceBuyer-facing product price on the retailer store.
Automatic paymentsCollective can debit retailer Shopify Payments and credit supplier Shopify Payments when enabled.

1. Retailer product import and publication

Retailers import supplier products from price lists, publish them to sales channels, and rely on Collective for supplier-backed inventory and price synchronization.

Products + Shipping 
flowchart TD
    A["Retailer opens the Collective app"] --> B["Browse connected supplier price lists"]
    B --> C["Select products to import<br/>or apply import policies"]
    C --> D["Collective creates products in the retailer store<br/>with Shopify Collective and supplier tags"]
    D --> E["Supplier content is copied<br/>title, description, media, category, SKU, barcode, weight, origin, HS code"]
    E --> F["Retailer publishes products to sales channels"]

    F --> G{"Inventory behavior"}
    G --> G1["Retailer available quantity is populated from supplier inventory available for online sales"]
    G --> G2["Purchases on supplier or connected retailer stores decrease shared available quantity"]
    G --> G3["Shopify checkout checks supplier inventory to protect against overselling"]

    F --> H{"Shipping behavior"}
    H --> H1["Imported products default to the Shopify Collective shipping profile"]
    H --> H2["Collective Carrier Service can return the same rates<br/>the supplier charges the retailer"]
    H --> H3["Retailer can move products to another shipping profile<br/>and define buyer-facing shipping rates"]

    F --> I{"Price behavior"}
    I --> I1["Supplier cost price is always synced from the price list"]
    I --> I2["Retail price can be retailer-managed<br/>or synchronized from the supplier"]
    I --> I3["Retailer discounts reduce retailer profit<br/>but do not change supplier cost price"]
  • Inventory is populated from supplier inventory available for online sales and propagated across connected stores.
  • Shopify checkout checks supplier inventory to help protect against overselling.
  • Cost price and inventory are always supplier-synced; buyer-facing retail price and shipping can be retailer-managed depending on settings.

4. Retailer order flow

After payment and fraud checks, Collective links retailer fulfillment orders with supplier orders and routes fulfillment to suppliers.

Orders + ERP 
sequenceDiagram
    participant Cust as Customer
    participant Ret as Retailer Store
    participant Checkout as Shopify Checkout
    participant COL as Shopify Collective
    participant Sup as Supplier Store
    participant SP as Shopify Payments

    Cust->>Ret: Start checkout
    Ret->>Checkout: Submit cart with Collective products
    Checkout->>COL: Check supplier inventory during checkout
    alt Supplier inventory is unavailable
        Checkout-->>Cust: Ask customer to adjust quantity or remove item
    else Supplier inventory is available
        Cust->>Ret: Place paid order
        Ret-->>COL: Retailer order created with Shopify Collective tag
        COL->>COL: Wait for payment and fraud checks<br/>about 2 minutes
        COL->>Sup: Send fulfillment request and create linked supplier order
        COL->>Ret: Add supplier tag and supplier order details to retailer order
        COL->>SP: Debit retailer Shopify Payments account<br/>when Automatic Payments are ON
    end

    Note over Ret,Sup: Supplier fulfills directly to the end customer, not to the retailer.
    Note over COL: Collective handles order splitting, payment routing, and shipping label generation for Collective items.
  • Retailer orders use the Shopify Collective tag and can include {$supplier_name}.
  • Supplier orders use the Shopify Collective and {$retailer_name} tags.
  • Suppliers are paid after fulfillment when Automatic Payments are enabled.

5. Supplier fulfillment and payout

Suppliers fulfill directly to the customer. Collective syncs fulfillment and can credit supplier Shopify Payments after fulfillment.

Fulfillment 
sequenceDiagram
    participant Sup as Supplier Store
    participant COL as Shopify Collective
    participant Ret as Retailer Store
    participant Cust as Customer
    participant SP as Shopify Payments

    COL->>Sup: Supplier order / fulfillment request is available
    Sup->>Sup: Process order in Shopify, ERP, OMS, or WMS
    Sup->>Sup: Create fulfillment and tracking details
    Sup-->>COL: Fulfillment update
    COL->>Ret: Sync fulfillment status and tracking to retailer order
    Ret-->>Cust: Send customer-facing shipping notification if configured
    COL->>SP: Credit supplier Shopify Payments account<br/>when Automatic Payments are ON

    Note over COL,SP: For partial fulfillments, payment adjustments follow fulfilled items. Validate exact shipping and tax treatment from Shopify Payments adjustments.

FAQ

Common integration and operations questions answered from the Markdown source and public Shopify references.

Source + Help Center

When is order data created in the supplier store?

The retailer order is created first. After payment, Collective waits about 2 minutes for fraud checks. If the order is not on hold, Collective sends the fulfillment request to the supplier, links the retailer fulfillment order with the supplier order, and the supplier sees the order in Shopify admin. External ERP, OMS, WMS, or finance sync should use order creation and order update webhooks on the relevant store.

How is the supplier order amount determined?

The supplier-side amount is based on supplier cost price, supplier shipping cost charged to the retailer, and applicable taxes or adjustments. It is not the customer's full checkout total. Validate exact amounts from supplier orders, order notes or custom details, and Shopify Payments adjustments. External finance or ERP systems should use order webhooks as the change feed, then reconcile against payment adjustment records.

How is inventory synchronized between supplier and retailer?

Supplier inventory available for online sales populates retailer available inventory. Purchases on the supplier store or connected retailer stores decrease the shared quantity, and Shopify checkout checks supplier inventory during checkout. External systems that mirror imported retailer products should use product creation, update, and deletion webhooks.

Can supplier and retailer inventory quantities diverge?

Yes, temporary divergence can happen while updates propagate. Average sync time is less than 30 seconds but can take up to 5 minutes. Checkout inventory checks protect Shopify checkout, but not manual order insertion or broken product/location setup.

Must product master data always be registered on the supplier side?

The product must exist in the supplier Shopify store, be active, be published to the Collective Supplier channel, and be included in a Collective price list. The upstream source can still be an ERP, PIM, or other external system. After retailer import, downstream retailer systems can use product creation, update, and deletion webhooks.

What happens to retailer data and order amounts if the supplier changes product data, price, or inventory?

Inventory and cost price are continuously synced from the supplier. Retail price follows the supplier only when retail price sync is enabled. Other product details can be copied and optionally kept in sync depending on product policy and sync settings. Future orders can use updated supplier cost, supplier shipping, retail price sync settings, and inventory availability; existing paid orders keep order-time amounts unless changed by order edits, cancellations, refunds, returns, or payment adjustments. Retailer-side integrations should use product webhooks when they need to mirror those changes externally.

What happens to supplier data and order amounts if the retailer changes product data, price, or inventory?

Retailer-side edits do not update the supplier source product. Retailers can edit buyer-facing details such as retail price, title, description, and media, but supplier-managed fields remain controlled by the supplier and some edits can be overwritten by supplier updates. Retailer-side price edits, discounts, and content edits do not change supplier cost price or supplier master data; supplier order amounts are still based on supplier cost price, supplier shipping, taxes, and order or payment adjustments. External systems connected to the retailer store should treat product webhooks as retailer-store updates, not supplier master-data changes.

What happens to the supplier order amount if the retailer configures a discount or sells below the supplier-side amount?

Retailer discounts and buyer-facing price changes affect what the customer pays and retailer profit, but they do not change supplier cost price. If the retailer discounts the item or sells below the supplier-side cost amount, the retailer can create a lower or negative margin while still owing the supplier based on supplier cost price. A 100 USD retail price with a 70 USD cost price still owes the supplier 70 USD even when a 20 USD customer discount is applied.

For orders paid with Shopify Payments, does the amount deposited to the supplier match the supplier order amount?

Not always as a one-to-one bank deposit. Automatic payments debit the retailer balance and credit the supplier balance after fulfillment, but payouts can aggregate multiple payments and be affected by refunds, returns, cancellations, reversals, shipping, taxes, timing, and balance behavior. External finance systems should combine order webhooks, return/refund webhooks, and payment adjustment reconciliation.
